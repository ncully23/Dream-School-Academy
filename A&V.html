Area & Volume

Overview

Most area and volume questions are formula-based. Master the formulas and you’ll solve nearly all easy and medium problems. Harder problems add layers of setup but still rely on the same core formulas.

Advanced Problem Types

  • Scaling: Understand how dimension changes affect area (squared) and volume (cubed).
  • Backsolving: Find missing dimensions when area or volume is given.
  • Coordinates: Use distance formula and base-height triangles to calculate area.
  • Shape in a Shape: Handle composite or inscribed figures, subtract overlapping volumes.
  • Shared Shape Faces: Combine figures while adjusting for overlapping surface area.

1. Area of 2D Shapes

  • Rectangles & Squares: A = l × w
  • Triangles: A = ½ × b × h
  • Circles: A = π × r²
  • Composite Figures: Break into known shapes or subtract areas

2. Surface Area of 3D Shapes

  • Cubes & Prisms: Add up all face areas
  • Cylinders, Cones, Spheres: Apply lateral/total surface area formulas
  • Real-World Applications: Packaging, coverings, and dimensions

3. Volume of 3D Shapes

  • Prisms & Cylinders: V = Bh or V = πr²h
  • Pyramids & Cones: V = ⅓Bh
  • Spheres: V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
  • Composite Solids: Combine or subtract volumes

4. Applications & Transformations

  • Scaling Shapes: Apply coefficients to formulas; area grows with square, volume with cube
  • Relative Dimensions: Use algebra to work with variables like "height is 4 more than radius"
  • Similarity: All linear dimensions scale equally to preserve shape
  • Shared Faces: Subtract or add shared surface area or volume accordingly
