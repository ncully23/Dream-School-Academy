📐 Triangle Trig — What is tan A?

Right triangle ABC is shown. What is the value of tan A?

A. \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{54} \)
B. \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \)
C. \( \sqrt{3} \)
D. \( 27\sqrt{3} \)

✅ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Analyze the Triangle
  • Triangle \( ABC \) has a right angle at \( C \), and angle \( B = 30^\circ \).
  • That means angle \( A = 60^\circ \), since the angles in a triangle sum to \( 180^\circ \).
  • The hypotenuse \( AB \) is given as 54.
Step 2: Use 30-60-90 Triangle Ratios
  • In a 30°-60°-90° triangle, side ratios are:
    • Opposite 30°: \( x \)
    • Opposite 60°: \( x\sqrt{3} \)
    • Hypotenuse: \( 2x \)
  • Since the hypotenuse is 54, set \( 2x = 54 \Rightarrow x = 27 \).
  • This gives:
    • \( BC = 27 \) (opposite 30°)
    • \( AC = 27\sqrt{3} \) (opposite 60°)
Step 3: Apply the Tangent Definition
  • Recall: \( \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} \)
  • For angle \( A = 60^\circ \):
    • Opposite: \( BC = 27\sqrt{3} \)
    • Adjacent: \( AC = 27 \)
  • \( \tan A = \frac{27\sqrt{3}}{27} = \sqrt{3} \)
✅ Final Answer: Choice C. \( \sqrt{3} \)
