Lines, Angles, and Triangles

1. Interior Angle Sum

All triangles have interior angles that sum to 180°.
For polygons: sum = \((n - 2) \times 180^\circ\), where \(n\) is the number of sides.

2. Complementary & Supplementary Angles

  • Complementary angles add to 90°
  • Supplementary angles add to 180°

3. Vertical Angles

Vertical angles are equal (opposite angles made by two intersecting lines).

4. Exterior Angle Rule

The exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two remote interior angles.

5. Triangle Angle & Side Properties

  • Sides are proportional to their opposite angles.
  • Isosceles triangles have two equal angles and sides.
  • 30-60-90 triangles follow a special ratio.

6. Alternate Interior & Corresponding Angles

When parallel lines are cut by a transversal, alternate interior and corresponding angles are equal.

7. Similar Triangles

  • AA, SSS, SAS are criteria for triangle similarity.
  • Parallel line extension creates similar triangles.
  • SSA is not a valid similarity rule.
  • Special right triangles include 45-45-90, 30-60-90, and Pythagorean triples.
