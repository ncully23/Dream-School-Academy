Lines, Angles, and Triangles: 11 Core Rules

1. Interior Angles in a Triangle
The three interior angles of a triangle always add up to 180°.
2. Exterior Angles
An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.
3. Sides and Angles
Larger angles face longer sides. In isosceles triangles, the base angles are equal.
4. Alternate Interior Angles
Formed when a transversal crosses parallel lines. These angles are congruent (equal).
5. Corresponding Angles
When a transversal cuts parallel lines, the angles in the same position are congruent.
6. Supplementary Angles
Two angles that add up to 180°. Often seen when two angles form a straight line.
7. Vertical Angles
When two lines cross, the opposite (vertical) angles are congruent.
8. Complementary Angles
Two angles that add up to 90°. Seen in right angles or right triangles.
9. Triangle Similarity
Use AA, SSS, or SAS to prove triangle similarity. Similar triangles have the same shape, and their sides are in proportion.
10. Scaling Similar Triangles
Similar triangles have equal angles and proportional sides. Shared angles in interior and exterior triangles often indicate similarity.
11. Using Proportions
Use ratios of corresponding sides in similar triangles to solve for unknown side lengths.
