Arc Length

  • Arc length represents the distance along part of the circle’s edge.
  • Formula (degrees): \( \text{Arc Length} = \frac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi r \)
  • Formula (radians): \( \text{Arc Length} = r\theta \)

Tangent Lines

  • A tangent line touches the circle at exactly one point.
  • It is always perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
  • Often tested in problems involving right triangles formed by radii and tangents.

Using Arc Length and Radius

  • To find the fraction of the circumference represented by an arc:
    • Fraction = Arc Length / Circumference
    • Circumference = 2πr
    • Then use the fraction to find angle measures or areas.
  • Can also reverse-engineer the angle or radius if arc length and one other value are known.
