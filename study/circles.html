II. The Concepts You Must Master

1) Radius & Isosceles Triangle Fact
  • Radius: distance from the center to any point on the circle.
  • Two radii to different points + the chord between them form an isosceles triangle.
2) Convert Radians ⇄ Degrees
  • \\(\\theta_{\\text{deg}} = \\theta_{\\text{rad}}\\cdot \\dfrac{180^\\circ}{\\pi}\\)
  • \\(\\theta_{\\text{rad}} = \\theta_{\\text{deg}}\\cdot \\dfrac{\\pi}{180^\\circ}\\)

Quick Converter

3) Circumference
  • \\(C = 2\\pi r\\)
4) Area
  • \\(A = \\pi r^2\\)
5) Arc Length
  • Radians: \\(S = r\\theta\\) (\\(\\theta\\) in radians)
  • Degrees: \\(\\dfrac{S}{C} = \\dfrac{\\theta_{\\text{deg}}}{360^\\circ}\\) → \\(S = \\dfrac{\\theta_{\\text{deg}}}{360^\\circ}\\cdot 2\\pi r\\)
  • \\(\\theta\\) is the central angle.

Quick Arc Calculator

6) Circle Equation & Completing the Square
  • Standard: \\((x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2\\)
  • Center: \\((h,k)\\); point on circle: \\((x,y)\\)
  • Complete the square to convert \\(x^2+y^2+Dx+Ey+F=0\\) to standard.

Mini CTS Helper

7) Tangents
  • Tangent at \\(P\\) ⟂ radius \\(\\overline{CP}\\).
  • If slope of radius is \\(m_{\\text{rad}}\\), then (when defined) \\(m_{\\text{tan}} = -\\dfrac{1}{m_{\\text{rad}}}\\).
  • Vertical radius → horizontal tangent; horizontal radius → vertical tangent.

Slope Helper

Point P should be on the circle; we compute slopes only.

Don’t forget these prerequisites:

  • Lines: slope, point–slope form, perpendicular/parallel, distance from point to line.
  • Angles & Triangles: sums, isosceles facts, central vs. inscribed angles.
  • Right Triangles & Trig: Pythagorean theorem, 30–60–90 & 45–45–90, SOH–CAH–TOA.
