🧩 Form, Structure, and Sense

  • Subject–Modifier Placement Modifiers must directly precede or follow the noun they describe
    • Correct: “Despite being cheap… commercial plastics…”
    • Incorrect: “Despite being cheap… two problems are…” (misleading)
  • Subject–Verb Agreement Singular subjects = singular verbs, plural subjects = plural verbs
    • “Frameworks underlie…” (plural subject, plural verb)
    • “Amplifying was…” (gerunds are always singular)
  • Finite vs. Nonfinite Verbs Use infinitives or participles for phrases, finite verbs for main clauses
    • Correct: “to forge a tool that…” (infinitive phrase)
    • Incorrect: “forging a tool…” (confusing structure without main clause)
  • Dangling Modifiers Modifier must clearly apply to the subject that follows
    • Correct: “Knowing this, the researchers found…”
    • Incorrect: “Knowing this, the moths found…” (illogical)
  • Logical Subject Placement Introductory phrases must be followed by the right subject
    • Correct: “In assessing the films… many critics…”
    • Incorrect: “In assessing the films… Kurosawa’s sources…”
  • Common Mistakes What to avoid in structure-based questions
    • Misplaced modifiers leading to ambiguity
    • Inconsistent or mismatched verb forms
    • Redundancy or awkward repetition
    • Inversion or fragment errors caused by misordered parts
  • Tips for Success How to avoid logic and structure issues
    • Find the subject immediately after introductory phrases
    • Use parallel structure when comparing or listing
    • Match noun and verb in form and clarity
    • Eliminate options that sound clunky or misordered
